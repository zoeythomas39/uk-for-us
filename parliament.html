Parliament

Parliament in the UK is made up of three parts. The bicameral, or two-part, legislature, includes the House of Lords and the House of Commons. The third part of Parliament is also the oldest part: the Crown.

The House of Commons is the elected chamber of Parliament. Representatives from 650 constituencies across England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland (see Geography) are elected every five years. The leader of the party with the most members elected becomes the Prime Minister. The party with the second-most members becomes the Opposition. Today, the Labour Party, which has a more socially progressive ideology, dominates the House of Commons with 411 seats. The Conservative Party is the Opposition party with 121 seats. The Commons makes decisions on taxes, debates big political issues and proposes new laws.
