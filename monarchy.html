Monarchy

The British Monarchy is constitutional, meaning that, while the king or queen is Head of State, only the elected Parliament can make and pass laws.

The monarch's main function as head of state are:

  • Appoint the Prime Minister and all other ministers
  • Open new sessions of Parliament
  • Give royal assent to bills passed by Parliament
  • Meet weekly with the Prime Minister
  • Receive ambassadors

The monarch is also head of the nation, which is a representative role to foster unity and pride.

He or she also acts as commander-in-chief of the British Armed Forces. (Source: royal.uk.)

The current Sovereign is King Charles III. He took the throne after Queen Elizabeth II's death in 2022 following 70 years of rule.

Next in line to the throne is the current Prince of Wales, Prince William, followed by his children, Prince George, Princess Charlotte and Prince Louis. (Source: royal.uk.)
