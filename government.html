Government

Unlike in the United States, "government" does in the United Kingom does not include the legislature. Instead, the term only refers to the Prime Minister, his Cabinet and his ministers.

The Prime Minister leads the government and is responsible for all policies and decisions.

The Prime Minister is not elected directly by the people. Instead, the politicacl party who wins the most seats in the House of Commons each general election selects their leader to serve as Prime Minister.

The current Prime Minister as of July 2024 is Sir Keir Starmer, a Labour Party leader who previously serve in the House of Commons.

The PM oversees the Civil Service and is the principal government figure in the House of Commons. They also get to choose other party members to work in the Government with them. These appointees become Cabinet ministers and junior ministers.

The government is responsible for deciding how the country is run day-to-day. They make decisions about the National Health Service, police and armed forces, welfare benefits like the State Pension and the UK's energy supply.
